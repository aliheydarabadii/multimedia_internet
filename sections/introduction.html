IP networks operate on a best-effort basis, meaning they don't provide guarantees for:

  • Delivery time
  • Packet delivery
  • Packet order
Feature TCP UDP
On Packet Sequence Guarantees order No guarantee
On Packet Delivery Guarantees delivery No guarantee
On Delay No guarantee No guarantee

TCP Characteristics

  • Connection-oriented protocol
  • Reliable data delivery
  • Flow control and congestion control
  • Sequenced data transmission
  • Error checking and recovery

UDP Characteristics

  • Connectionless protocol
  • Fast data transmission
  • No flow control
  • No guaranteed delivery
  • Ideal for real-time applications

Both TCP and UDP rely on IP's best-effort service, but they handle the lack of guarantees differently based on their application requirements.
